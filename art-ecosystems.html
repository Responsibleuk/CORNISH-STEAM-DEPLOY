About

Cornish STEAM is proud to present Art Ecosystems, a project that explores the fusion of art and science, technology, and mathematics to revolutionize the way we approach innovation. Our Cornish-based project recognizes that incorporating art into these fields can lead to more creative and innovative solutions to real-world challenges.

We believe that creativity and innovation are essential components of problem-solving, and that a holistic approach to innovation that includes art can enhance the user experience. Art Ecosystems also recognizes the impact of art on public engagement with projects. By highlighting the artistic elements, we can make these projects more accessible and interesting to a wider audience. We draw inspiration from the impact of art on architecture, where functional buildings are also works of art, inspiring and delighting those who view them.

As a Cornish-based STEAM organization, we are committed to exploring the possibilities of this approach, and are excited to see the innovative projects that will result from the marriage of art and science, technology, engineering and mathematics. Join us in discovering the art of innovation and transforming the way we approach problem-solving.

Jonathan Middlemiss

Jonathan Middlemis (1949-2021) Was a visual artist, potter, painter, sculpture and teacher. He studied at Scarborough College of Art and Exeter College of Art. He honed his ceramics working in pottery production in Devon, Cornwall and Somerset. His work has been exhibited since 1979 in the UK and in the USA, but his first solo show was in 1990 at  Keramik-Museum of Frechen, Keramik-Museum of Kellinghusen, and  Kreissparkasse, Hannover.

In 1988-1993 he did a lecture tour of California, Arizona. Workshop for Association of Californian Ceramic Artists, San Francisco & Azusa Pacific College, Los Angeles. California, San Diego and New York. After lecturing at Falmouth School of Art, Middlemiss became a full time ceramics tutor 1995-2005 . Later teaching ceramics in Further Education at Brunel University 2012-18.

His ceramic work can be found in public collections and museums around the world. Over a career of 40 years he has had many solo exhibitions in Britain, Germany, France, Holland, Belgium, Italy, Switzerland, the Middle East, America, Taiwan, Japan, Korea and New Zealand.

Dynamic interaction between structural surface and decoration, ceramic form as sculpture effortlessly transforms from physical to digital and extended reality. The geometric three dimensional shapes produced in stoneware are strikingly projected into the digital realm.
